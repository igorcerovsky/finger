  Interpretation:
  The +Ld contact penalty (+25–35% A2) always works against long fingers.
  The +Lp+Lm angle advantage (−50% A2) always works for long fingers.
  For REALISTIC athletes (both Ld and Lp+Lm are longer), the angle
  advantage dominates and long fingers still have lower A2 in isolation.

  The observed SHORT-CLIMBER dominance is therefore NOT primarily a
  single-finger pulley-load effect. The body-size advantage operates
  through several reinforcing routes:
    1. Lower absolute body weight → lower grip force required per hold.
    2. More favourable strength-to-weight ratio (smaller cross-section
       scales as L^2, weight as L^3 → smaller athletes are relatively
       stronger per kg at the same training state).
    3. Reach efficiency on steep terrain: shorter trunk + proportionally
       longer arms (common in elite climbers) improves hip-to-hold distance.
    4. On SMALL HOLDS specifically, Ld contact penalty reduces the long-
       finger angle advantage: the net A2 benefit shrinks from ~50% to ~40%,
       not enough to flip the advantage but enough to narrow it.
    5. Gymnasts are short for the same L^2/L^3 strength-to-weight reason.
