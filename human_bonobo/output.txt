======================================================
   MODEL COMPARISON: 3D Climbing vs PeerJ Human       
======================================================

--- 1. Running Our 3D Model ('climbing_finger_3d.py') ---
-> Our Model (EMG Constrained) - FDP: 211.7 N, FDS: 176.4 N
-> Our Model (Direct 3x3)      - FDP: 204.8 N, FDS: 185.1 N

--- 2. Running PeerJ Model ('Fingermodel.py') ---
-> PeerJ Model (Min Stress SQP) - FDP: 137.5 N, FDS: 189.0 N

------------------------------------------------------
   ANALYSIS OF DIFFERENCES                            
------------------------------------------------------
1. Muscle Recruitment Paradigm:
   - Our 3D Model: Uses exact biological EMG sharing ratios (Vigouroux 2006).
   - PeerJ Model: Uses Static Optimization (minimizing squared muscle stress).
     This leads the PeerJ model to favor FDS strongly because its moment arms 
     at PIP and MCP are superior, mathematically avoiding FDP when possible 
     even though climbers intrinsically recruit FDP to stabilize the DIP joint.

2. Geometry & Extensor Mechanism:
   - Our 3D Model: Simplifies extensor mechanism to focus on primary flexors 
     with lateral friction and exact crimp contact point shifting.
   - PeerJ Model: A full hand model including intrinsic muscles (Lumbricals, 
     Interossei) and the entire Extensor Hood mechanism via routing fractions. 
     The external force causes co-contraction in these stabilizers, 
     altering the net flexor demand.
